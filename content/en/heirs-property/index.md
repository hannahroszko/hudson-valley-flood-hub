\# Proving Ownership Without a Deed

If you inherited your home but your name is not on the deed, FEMA now accepts alternative documents. You only need \*\*ONE\*\* of the following to qualify for home repair assistance:



\### 1. Public Official’s Statement

A written statement from a local public official (such as a Mayor, Police Chief, or Postmaster) that names you as the owner and states you have lived there for at least one year.



\### 2. Major Repair Receipts

Copies of receipts for significant maintenance or repairs (e.g., roof, plumbing, HVAC) made to the home within the last \*\*5 years\*\*.



\### 3. Property Tax Documents

A property tax bill or receipt for the home, even if it is in the name of the deceased owner.



\### 4. Legal Documents

A will, a death certificate with a letter of administration, or an Affidavit of Heirship.



\### 5. Self-Declarative Statement

As a last resort, a signed statement explaining your ownership status under penalty of perjury.
